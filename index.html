Location & Transportation

Schenströmska Herrgården in Ramnäs, Sweden

We are delighted to celebrate this special weekend with our family and friends at Schenströmska Herrgården. This historic mansion, built in the early 1700s and completed in 1762, provides the perfect setting for both our wedding and the Midsummer celebration. The best part? Everyone can stay on-site throughout the weekend! For more information about the venue, visit: https://schenstromska.se/konferens-vasteras/.

How to Get to Schenströmska Herrgården

By Car:
Enter the following address into Google Maps or your GPS: Schenströms Väg 1, 730 60 Ramnäs. Parking is available on-site, including three charging poles for electric vehicles (one regular and two Tesla-compatible).

By Air:
Fly to Stockholm Arlanda Airport, take a train to Västerås, then transfer to a train heading to Ramnäs station. Tickets can be purchased in advance through SJ. From Ramnäs station, it’s a 30-minute walk to the venue, or you can request a pickup (please ensure this is noted in your RSVP). Alternatively, you can rent a car at the airport.

By Public Transportation:
Take a train to Ramnäs station (usually via Västerås). From the station, it’s a 30-minute walk to the wedding location, or you can request a pickup (please ensure this is noted in your RSVP).

Paying in Sweden and Currency Information

If you're traveling from abroad, it's good to know that Sweden’s currency is the Swedish Krona (SEK). While Sweden has not adopted the Euro, debit and credit cards are widely accepted, including Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. However, cash payments in Euros or other foreign currencies are not possible in Sweden.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What should I bring in terms of outfits?
    • Formal attire for the wedding ceremony.
    • Floral or white clothing for the Midsummer celebration.
    • Swimwear and towel for the sauna and swimming, flip-flops recommended.
    • Comfortable sportswear (including shoes) for the Midsummer games.
    • A flower crown, if possible (you can prepare or purchase one in advance – midsommarkrans.
  2. Can I bring a date?

    Unless specified on your invitation, bringing a date isn’t permitted. However, if you’d like to discuss this, please contact Albin and Yvonne directly at: brollop.kraak.persson@gmail.com

  3. Are kids allowed?

    Yes! If you need a baby bed (free of charge, however, only 2 available), please indicate this in your RSVP or contact Albin and Yvonne directly at:brollop.kraak.persson@gmail.com

  4. Will transportation be provided?

    Transportation will be arranged, as much as possible, from Ramnäs station to the wedding location.

  5. Will there be free parking?

    Yes, free parking is available, including three charging poles for electric cars (two for Tesla and one regular).

  6. What if I have dietary restrictions?

    Please mention any dietary restrictions in the RSVP under the designated section.

  7. What is the drinks situation?

    We’ll provide an open bar during the dinners and parties. If you’d like to bring any special schnapps, or drink from your country, or other drinks, feel free to do so!

  8. Can I bring pets?

    Unfortunately, no. Although we love animals, this event will be pet-free.

  9. When should I arrive?

    Friday, June 20th at 15:30.

  10. When does it all end?

    Sunday, June 22nd at 11:00.

  11. Is the venue accessible for people with disabilities?

    Overall, the venue is accessible.

    • The wedding ceremony will be held outdoors and is easily accessible.
    • The dinner will take place in the “stables,” which also have ample accessibility.
    • Most bedrooms are not wheelchair accessible, with the exception of one, which has been reserved for Albin’s mother.
  12. Is there Wi-Fi available?

    Yes, Wi-Fi is accessible throughout the premises.

  13. What about gifts?

    Your presence for this special weekend is all we truly wish for! However, if you’d like to give a gift, we would be grateful for an envelope contribution to help sponsor our future travels.

  14. What are the Midsummer games?

    Midsummer is the longest day of the year and has long been considered a magical time. Traditionally, Swedes celebrate Midsummer to welcome summer and the season of fertility. The quintessential Midsummer celebration includes:

    • Wearing flowers in your hair.
    • Outdoors games such as kubb, boot throwing, the wheelbarrow, and more!
    • Dancing around a maypole.
    • Singing traditional songs while drinking flavored schnapps.

    All in all, it’s a grand day out filled with joy and laughter!
